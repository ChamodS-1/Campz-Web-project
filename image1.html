DISCRIPTION

The Ruwanweli Maha Seya, also known as the Mahathupa (the Great Thupa),
is a stupa (a hemispherical structure containing relics) in Anuradhapura, Sri Lanka.
Two quarts or one Dona of the Buddha's relics are enshrined in the stupa,
making it the largest collection of his relics anywhere. It was built by Sinhalese King Dutugemunu
in c. 140 B.C., who became king of Sri Lanka after a war in which the Chola King ElƒÅra (Ellalan)
was defeated. It is also known as Swarnamali Seya. The stupa is one of the world's tallest
ancient monuments, standing at 103 m (338 ft) and with a circumference of 290 m (951 ft).
